What students said

Four themes from the open-ended responses

Synthesized from the three free-text questions on added information, desired changes, and pacing.

Tie every pop-up back to Jane

Learners wanted contextual data points (immigration rates, transit times) to be explicitly connected to Jane's daily life rather than presented as standalone facts.

"Keep the narrative tied to Jane — e.g. 'this is Jane's route to the doctor's office...' — I wasn't sure how some pop-ups specifically tied to older adults."
"I was unclear on what the challenges were, so be more specific."
Give learners more agency

Several students wanted to move through the environment and make choices, rather than follow a fixed path.

"I suggest giving the option to move about the environment and make choices."
"Being able to move around on your own could help — especially seeing storefronts and whether the neighborhood had nutritious food options."
Build confidence before talking to Jane

The jump straight into a live conversation left some learners nervous and under-prepared.

"I would want a practice round before actually talking to the patient. I had no confidence and was nervous."
"When interacting with Jane, I wished there was a clipboard of her history for us."
Pacing is close, but a bit long in places

Most learners felt the length was appropriate, but several flagged specific clips that ran past the point where they'd finished reading the pop-ups.

"The video clips were not too long, but I wish it moved on once each pop-up was completed."
"Number of tour scenes were good. Video clips were a little too long."
Recommendations

Suggested next iterations

Drawn directly from the quantitative dips and qualitative themes above.

  • 1
    Connect every pop-up explicitly to Jane's storyReframe standalone statistics as consequences for Jane's routine, so their relevance to older-adult challenges is unmistakable.
  • 2
    Add a low-stakes practice round before the live conversationA short warm-up exchange with Jane would address the confidence gap several learners named directly.
  • 3
    Auto-advance clips once pop-ups are cleared, and offer 2x playbackTargets the most common pacing complaint without shortening the tour overall.
  • 4
    Add a brief onboarding moment for first-time VR usersA short orientation before immersion would help the ~35% who hadn't used VR recently.
  • 5
    Introduce light navigational choiceLetting learners look around storefronts or choose a path could deepen the sense of agency without a full open-world rebuild.
