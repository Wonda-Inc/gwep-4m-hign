Open-Ended Feedback

Selected representative responses from the free-text questions, lightly condensed. Full response sets are available on request.

Recurring theme: learners consistently asked for more freedom of movement, tighter linkage between pop-up facts and Jane's story, and a short practice round before the home-visit conversation.
Q9 — What could be added to help better understand the challenges?
34 responses collected

It may be helpful to make the pop-ups very focused to the experience of older adults — I wasn't always sure how a fact tied back to Jane specifically.

I think being able to move around on your own would help, especially to see whether the neighborhood had nutritious stores available.

Maybe more prompts at the beginning for the user to be mindful of before immersing in the setting.

I wish there were pointers showing where the pop-ups came up.

Q11 — If you could change one thing about the VR tour, what would it be?
34 responses collected

I would want a practice round before actually talking to the patient — I had no confidence and was nervous.

I suggest giving the option to move about the environment and make choices.

Maybe a few minutes at the beginning to get used to the VR experience, since it was my first time in many years.

I think having more interactive sections during the walkthrough could help the learner.

Q25 — Was the tour too long? Were the video clips too long or too short?
34 responses collected

The video clips were not too long, but I wish it moved on once each pop-up was completed.

Some of the clips were too long — I had finished looking around and reading the pop-ups, but the clip still had a while left.

It felt like perfect timing; I learned a lot during the scenes and liked the descriptions.

Number of tour scenes were good; video clips were a little too long.
